Gating the RW-1 correction gradient: the complete P1–P5 experimental record

Companion to the Proposal 1–5 Results summary page: the full record of the study, from the thesis reproduction it stands on, through the warm-up config fix that overturned the first verdict, to per-proposal results, robustness runs, and the shape-spectrum generalization test.

Bottom line

  • The first "gating never helps" verdict was a config artifact. With warm-up = plain RW-1 (gate off) then gate on, and neg_x init, all five proposals are competitive with the tuned RW-1.
  • P5 (temporal persistence) + auto-λ beats the best-HP/200ep RW-1 on GECCO in 6 of 6 repeat runs (min 0.661 > 0.639, mean 0.677). The only verdict-set win of any proposal, and it is reproducible.
  • No generalization. Across the anomaly-shape spectrum there is no consistent pattern; the win is GECCO-specific.

1Background and objective

Base method (RW-1). From Baldo's 2025 FEUP MSc thesis, "Read & Write Machine Learning for Outlier Detection in Multivariate Time Series": a DeepAnT-style CNN forecaster plus a separate trainable correction tensor, initialized as -X so the initial corrected input is a zero series. The model predicts the corrected series; the loss is RMSE(X+correction, Y+correction) plus an L1 sparsity penalty on the correction. Gradients w.r.t. the correction accumulate over each epoch and are applied once at epoch end via RMSprop (the Epoch-Wise strategy of Algorithm 2). The anomaly score is mean|correction| per timestep: points the model cannot predict keep large corrections.

Added idea (Auto-CEGAR). From our prior Auto-CEGAR work in the classification domain: a gate g = wrongness × confidence in [0,1] gives a per-window gradient scale s = 1 + λ·g, applied through ScaleGrad (identity forward, so the reported loss stays the true RMSE; only the backward gradient is multiplied). Auto-controllers adapt λ (tail-ratio controller) and τ (valley-detection controller) at each epoch end.

Research question. Does CEGAR-style gating of the RW-1 correction gradient, steering correction effort toward "confidently wrong" windows, improve unsupervised multivariate time-series anomaly detection over plain RW-1?

The five candidate gates

All five are thin subclasses of a common CNN_RW_CEGAR base (itself a subclass of the reproduced RW-1) overriding only the signal computation; warm-up, gate mechanics, ScaleGrad, controllers, and scoring are shared.

#NameGate signals
P1Residual-Gatedwrongness from a robust z-score of the residual: E_t = σ(k·(robust_z − τ)), robust_z = (r − median)/MAD; confidence = 1 (basic variant)
P2Uncertainty-AwareMC-dropout (M = 5 passes): uncertainty u_t, normalized error e_t = ‖Y − μ‖/√(u_t+ε); gate = high error AND low uncertainty
P3Correction-Consistencythe correction's own behaviour: magnitude d_t and epoch-to-epoch direction consistency v_t = cos(ΔC^e, ΔC^{e−1}); amplify + preserve write-back grad·(1 − γg)
P4Dual-Gate Residual + Gradientg_res (robust-z residual) × g_grad (robust-z of input-gradient norm; costs an extra forward+backward per batch)
P5Temporal-Persistencegate only errors that persist over neighbouring windows: m_t = 1(e_t > τ_e), p_t = mean(m_{t−h..t+h}), g = σ(k(e_t−τ_e)/mad) · σ(k_p(p_t−τ_p))

Two documented fixes were required to reproduce RW-1 faithfully:

  • Gradient activation = linear (identity), as in the Algorithm 2 pseudocode. The tabled ReLU interacts badly with the L1 gradient: with correction ≈ −X the L1 gradient sign is ≈ −1 everywhere, ReLU zeroes the combined gradient, and the correction freezes (score inversion on Genesis/GECCO/GHL).
  • l1_weight = 1e-3 instead of the implicit 1.0: torch.norm sums over all elements, so full-weight L1 crushes the correction to zero over 200 epochs and collapses the anomaly signal.

Standing caveat

Proposals run 100 epochs with default HP while the RW-1 reference is best-HP / 200 epochs, so every Δ is config-confounded on that axis. Magnitudes are indicative, not a like-for-like tune-off.

All runs are tracked in wandb (yoonmeeh-cmu/rwml-autocegar) with per-epoch train/* metrics, gate/correction interpretability metrics (gate/*, corr/*), and per-proposal summary runs; compute nodes log offline and are synced afterwards.

4The warm-up config fix

The first screening round used forecaster-only warm-up (correction OFF during warm-up) with correction_init='zero'. Under that config every proposal lost to RW-1, with deltas as large as −0.14 on GECCO: the original "P1 lost 0/3, gating never helps" verdict.

Diagnosis showed this was largely a config artifact, not a property of gating: starting the correction late and from zero deprives it of the training budget and the Algorithm-2 dynamics that plain RW-1 enjoys. The corrected config keeps warm-up but changes what warms up:

Corrected config: 10 epochs of plain RW-1 (correction training ON, gate OFF), then the CEGAR gate switches on; Algorithm-2-faithful neg_x init; 100 epochs total. Old runs archived in _backup_oldconfig/; corrected wandb runs regrouped.

Under the corrected config all five proposals became competitive with the tuned RW-1. The reversal is itself a substantive result: the initial all-negative verdict was a training-curriculum artifact.

8Generalization: the shape hypothesis fails

Hypothesis tested: "P5 wins on block-shaped anomalies." The 4-collection extension rejects it:

  • TAO (point): everyone at ~0.995, a saturated trivial tie.
  • MSL (block, weak baseline 0.131): P1/P2/P5 edge above by ~0.005, within no-seed noise.
  • SWaT (block, strong baseline 0.444): every proposal collapses to ~0.13–0.15, a heavy loss on exactly the shape the hypothesis predicts a win for.
  • PSM (mixed): all slightly below the baseline.
  • CreditCard (point, verdict set) also loses for everyone: correction-magnitude scoring is intrinsically weak for isolated point anomalies (RW-1 itself only reaches 0.111 there).

There is no clean shape-to-win rule. The P5 win is GECCO-specific.

9Conclusions

  1. The corrected config overturns the initial verdict. The early 0/N losses were largely a forecaster-only-warm-up + zero-init artifact; with warm-up = plain RW-1 then gate on, all five proposals are competitive with tuned RW-1.
  2. P5 + auto-λ robustly beats best-HP/200ep RW-1 on GECCO (6/6 runs, min 0.661 > 0.639, mean 0.677): a genuine, reproducible, scoped win, and evidence that the auto-λ controller adds value.
  3. The win does not generalize across the shape spectrum (SWaT lost badly, MSL within noise, point/mixed at or below baseline).
  4. Caveats: all deltas remain config-confounded (100ep/default-HP vs best-HP/200ep); verdict cells are single unseeded runs except the P5 robustness test. The honest next step before claiming P5 > RW-1 as a method is a matched-HP tune-off.

Net result: a scoped positive finding (P5 + auto-λ > tuned RW-1 on GECCO, reproducibly) on top of a corrected-config negative-results arc (no general improvement across shapes or collections), plus a methodological contribution: the warm-up confound diagnosis and a diagnostics suite that explains wins and losses via gate localization and correction concentration.
