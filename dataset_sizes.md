# Datasets (TSB-AD-M): size, runtime, and anomaly structure

Single reference for the TSB-AD-M corpus (N = 200 series, 17 collections) used in
the RW / RW-1 / Auto-CEGAR experiments. **Terminology follows Baldo's thesis**:
**N** = number of series, **Avg Len** = average series length, **Dim** =
dimensionality (a.k.a. *Mean Number of Channels* in the thesis Table 3.2). `cells`
= Avg Len × Dim is a derived compute-cost proxy (not a thesis metric).

Time = RW-1 training time (l1=0.001, 200 epochs, 1× V100); two columns: per-series
mean and **collection total** (= N × mean, wall-clock to run the whole collection
sequentially on one GPU).

## Overall

- **Avg Len (length)**: min 1,096 / median 46,655 / max 650,000
- **Dim (channels)**: min 2 / median 19 / max 248
- **cells (Len × Dim)**: min 30,000 / median 460,800 / max 20,395,869 (**680× spread**)

## Anomaly structure

By anomaly **shape** (labels only), all 200 series across 17 collections. Per-series detail in `experiments/proposals/dataset_anomaly_structure.csv`.

`shape`: **point-like** = median anomaly run ≤ 2 (isolated points); **block-like** = median run ≥ 20 (contiguous segments); **mixed** in between. Most collections are block-like (only creditcard/TAO are point-like, PSM mixed). Since P1–P3 show that any CEGAR gating of the correction does not beat RW-1 — and the point-like creditcard fails too — this block dominance suggests the negative result generalizes broadly across the corpus, not just the tested three. (No shape→failure causal claim is made: GECCO's largest drop is confounded with RW-1 being strongest there.)
